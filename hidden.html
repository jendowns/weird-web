Hidden Mother

 Unknown Photo, circa 1870s, via Wikimedia Commons (Public Domain) "Hidden mother photography" was common in the Victorian era. Due to long exposure times for daguerreotypes (ranging from several seconds to as long as a couple minutes), mothers would often hide under blankets or behind curtains to help keep a young child still. Sometimes photographs were even modified after the fact to remove the mother, making the image appear even more disturbing. There's something eerie about his genre of photograph.
